# Supplemental Claim Walkthrough — Form 20-0995

> **Educational use only. Not legal or claims advice.**
> *VVV Digitals · vvvdigitals.com*

---

## When to use Supplemental Claim

You file a Supplemental Claim when **you have new and relevant evidence** that wasn't in VA's file when they made the prior decision. VA owes you a "duty to assist" on Supplemental Claims — they'll order new exams or pull new records if your evidence supports it.

**"New and relevant" means:**
- New: not previously in your file
- Relevant: tends to prove or disprove a fact at issue

Examples:
- A private medical record VA hadn't seen
- A buddy statement
- A new C&P exam result
- A specialist's diagnosis
- DBQ filled out by a private provider
- VA records VA didn't pull from another facility

---

## Filing window — and why it matters

- **File within 1 year** of the prior decision to **preserve your effective date**
- **File after 1 year**: still allowed, but effective date may be the date of the new claim, not the original

> **CRITICAL:** If you're approaching the 1-year mark and not sure whether you have enough evidence, file an **Intent to File (ITF) — VA Form 21-0966**. That preserves your effective date for another year while you gather evidence.

---

## What you'll need

- [ ] A copy of the rating decision being supplemented
- [ ] All new evidence ready to submit (originals or clear scans)
- [ ] List of evidence you want VA to retrieve (private treatment records, federal records)
- [ ] Your reasoning: how does this new evidence change the prior outcome?

## Filling out Form 20-0995

### Section I — Veteran Information
Standard. Match VA's records exactly.

### Section II — Issue(s) you're claiming
- List each issue separately
- For each, identify the **prior decision date** you're supplementing

### Section III — New and relevant evidence
- Box A: Are you submitting evidence with this claim? **YES** if attaching anything.
- Box B: Are you identifying evidence VA should retrieve?
  - List each source: provider name, address, dates of treatment
  - Use VA Form 21-4142 to authorize private record retrieval

### Section IV — Treatment records
- Federal facilities (VA, DOD, etc.): VA pulls these automatically — list dates and locations
- Private facilities: must authorize via 21-4142

### Section V — Election to opt in
If your prior decision was a Statement of the Case (SOC) under the legacy appeals system, you can opt into the new (AMA) lanes here.

### Section VI — Signature
Sign and date.

## Strategic tips

### 1. Don't bury your evidence
If your strongest evidence is a single buddy letter, attach it as the FIRST exhibit. Reviewers read top-to-bottom.

### 2. Get a DBQ from a private provider
A Disability Benefits Questionnaire (DBQ) filled out by your treating physician is one of the strongest forms of evidence on a Supplemental Claim. VA cannot ignore an examiner's findings — they have to address them.

### 3. Use lay statements wisely
Buddy letters from spouses, parents, fellow service members carry weight on:
- Symptom observations (severity, frequency, triggers)
- In-service events (combat, MST, accidents)
- Continuity of symptoms since service

### 4. Stack evidence around a single rating criterion
Don't submit 200 pages of records hoping VA finds your case. Build a 5-page brief that explicitly walks through the rating criteria and shows which evidence proves which element.

## After filing

1. **Confirmation** — VA acknowledges within ~30 days
2. **Duty to assist** — VA orders any necessary exams/records
3. **C&P exam** — if needed, you may be scheduled (4–8 weeks)
4. **Decision** — typical turnaround: 4–6 months
5. **Outcome:**
   - Granted (with original effective date preserved if filed within 1 year)
   - Denied — next move: file another Supplemental with more evidence, or file HLR if you think VA misapplied the law

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Submitting evidence VA already has.** Not "new" — won't trigger duty to assist.
2. **Forgetting Form 21-4142** for private records. VA can't retrieve without authorization.
3. **Filing after 1 year unnecessarily.** Always file ITF if you're at the deadline.
4. **Not connecting evidence to the rating criteria.** Make it easy for the rater to grant.

## Sample cover statement

> **Re: Supplemental Claim — [Your Name], File Number [X]**
>
> I am submitting a Supplemental Claim with new and relevant evidence regarding the rating decision dated [DATE].
>
> **Issue 1: [Specific contention]**
>
> The prior decision found [SUMMARY]. I am submitting the following new and relevant evidence:
>
> - Exhibit A: [Description, source, date]
> - Exhibit B: [Description, source, date]
>
> This evidence is relevant because it [SPECIFIC CONNECTION TO RATING CRITERIA OR FACTUAL ELEMENT].
>
> **Requested action:** [SPECIFIC OUTCOME with effective date]
>
> [Repeat for each issue]
>
> Respectfully submitted,
> [Signature, date]
