# Decision Tree Cheat Sheet

> **Educational use only. Not legal or claims advice.**
> *VVV Digitals · vvvdigitals.com*

## You got a rating decision. Now what?

### Step 1: Do you agree with it?
- **Yes** → Done. Cash the retro check.
- **No** → Continue.

### Step 2: How long ago was the decision?

**Less than 1 year ago** → Continue.
**More than 1 year ago** → Skip to Step 6.

### Step 3: Do you have NEW evidence VA didn't have?

**Yes** → File a **Supplemental Claim (Form 20-0995)**.
- Effective date preserved
- VA owes you duty to assist
- Submit your new evidence with the form

**No** → Continue to Step 4.

### Step 4: Did VA misapply the law or misweigh the existing evidence?

**Yes** → File a **Higher-Level Review (Form 20-0996)**.
- No new evidence allowed
- Effective date preserved
- ALWAYS request the informal phone conference

**No** → Continue to Step 5.

### Step 5: Without new evidence and without a legal/factual error, neither HLR nor Supplemental will succeed.

**Recommended action:** Stop and gather evidence. Consider:
- New C&P-style exam from a private provider (DBQ)
- Buddy letters
- Treatment records VA hasn't pulled
- Then file Supplemental within the 1-year window

### Step 6: More than 1 year has passed since the decision

The decision is "final." Two paths remain:
- **Clear and Unmistakable Error (CUE)** — extremely high bar; consult an accredited rep
- **New claim** — file fresh with new evidence (effective date will be the new claim date, not the original)

## The 1-year rule, visually

```
DECISION DATE ─────────────────────────────┬────────────────► TIME
   │                                       │
   │ YOU HAVE 1 YEAR HERE                  │ DECISION IS
   │ to preserve effective date            │ FINAL after this
   │ via HLR / Supplemental / Appeal       │
```

> **CRITICAL:** Set a calendar reminder for day 350 of every decision. File something — even an ITF for a Supplemental — before the year is up.

## Choosing between HLR and Supplemental — at a glance

|  | **HLR (20-0996)** | **Supplemental (20-0995)** |
|---|---|---|
| New evidence allowed? | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Effective date preserved? | ✅ If within 1 yr | ✅ If within 1 yr |
| VA's duty to assist? | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Phone conference option? | ✅ Recommended | ❌ No |
| Typical decision time | 4–7 months | 4–6 months |
| When to use | Misapplied law / overlooked record | New medical, lay, or buddy evidence |

---

## Effective date quick reference

| You have... | Effective date will be... |
|---|---|
| ITF + formal claim within 1 year | ITF date |
| Increase claim + symptoms worsened within 1 year prior | Facts-found date (if proven) |
| Original claim only, no ITF | Date claim received |
| Supplemental within 1 year of decision | Original claim date preserved |
| HLR within 1 year of decision | Original claim date preserved |
| Anything filed more than 1 year out | Date of new filing (loses retro) |

---

## What to file by 1-year deadline if you're not ready

If you're approaching the 1-year mark and don't have everything together:

**File an Intent to File (Form 21-0966)** — gives you another year to perfect the claim while preserving the effective date.

---

## Three rules that override most situations

1. **Always request the HLR informal conference.** Free, fast, dramatically improves outcomes.
2. **Never throw away VSO confirmation letters or ITF receipts.** Worth tens of thousands later.
3. **File a Supplemental BEFORE the 1-year deadline if you have anything new.** "I might have evidence" beats "decision became final."
