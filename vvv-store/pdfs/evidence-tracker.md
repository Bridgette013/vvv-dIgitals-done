## Why a tracker matters

Your VA Claims File (C-file) often has hundreds — sometimes thousands — of pages. When you file an HLR or Supplemental and need to point to specific evidence, you need to find it fast. This tracker is your map.

---

## How to use this tracker

1. **Request your full C-file** — submit a Privacy Act request via VA.gov (allow 30–90 days)
2. **Open it as a single PDF** — number every page
3. **For each entry below, fill in the page numbers and any quotes**
4. When filing HLR/Supplemental, cite by page number: *"See C-file pp. 47, 51, 89"*

## Document type cheat sheet

| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Service treatment record (STR)** | In-service medical records, sick calls, line-of-duty determinations |

| **C&P exam** | Compensation & Pension exam reports |

## Missing evidence checklist

Things often missing from a C-file that may help your claim:

- [ ] Pre-service medical records (if condition existed before service or was noted at entrance exam)
- [ ] Service treatment records from deployment / TDY units (often filed separately)
- [ ] VA records from facilities other than your primary
- [ ] Private treatment records (require Form 21-4142 to authorize)
- [ ] Buddy statements from people who served with you
- [ ] Spouse/family statements about post-service symptom onset
- [ ] Vocational rehabilitation (Chapter 31) file
- [ ] DOL records if you've filed for SSA disability
- [ ] Workers comp records relating to a claimed condition
- [ ] Unit history / morning reports for stressor verification (PTSD claims)
