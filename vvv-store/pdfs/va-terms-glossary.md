# VA Terms Glossary — Plain English

> **Educational use only. Not legal or claims advice.**
> *VVV Digitals · vvvdigitals.com*

---

## Forms

**21-526EZ** — Application for Disability Compensation. The main form for filing or expanding a claim.

**21-0966** — Intent to File. Preserves your effective date for 1 year while you gather evidence.

**21-4142** — Authorization to Disclose Information. Lets VA pull your private medical records.

**21-4138** — Statement in Support of Claim. The free-text form for personal statements, buddy letters, etc.

**20-0995** — Decision Review Request: Supplemental Claim. New evidence lane.

**20-0996** — Decision Review Request: Higher-Level Review. No new evidence; review for legal/factual error.

**10182** — Notice of Disagreement (Board appeal). Used when you appeal directly to the Board of Veterans' Appeals.

**21P-0969** — Income & Asset Statement. Used for pension and SMC claims.

---

## Types of decisions

**Rating decision** — VA's written decision granting, denying, or modifying disability compensation.

**Codesheet** — The page summarizing your service-connected conditions, ratings, effective dates, and combined rating.

**Statement of the Case (SOC)** — A legacy-system document explaining VA's reasoning. Largely replaced by AMA but still exists for older claims.

**Supplemental Statement of the Case (SSOC)** — Updated SOC after new evidence is added.

---

## Rating concepts

**Rating** — A percentage (0–100, in 10% increments) reflecting how disabling a condition is.

**Combined rating** — How VA combines multiple ratings using the "VA math" formula in 38 CFR § 4.25 (it's NOT addition).

**Diagnostic Code (DC)** — A four-digit code in the rating schedule defining how a condition is rated. (Example: DC 9411 = PTSD)

**Bilateral factor** — Extra credit for paired-extremity disabilities (both knees, both arms, etc.).

**Pyramiding** — Rating the same disability under multiple codes. Generally not allowed (38 CFR § 4.14).

---

## Effective date concepts

**Effective date** — The date your benefit starts. Determines retroactive pay.

**Date of claim (DOC)** — The date VA received your claim or ITF.

**Facts-found date** — For increases: the date your condition worsened to the higher level (38 CFR § 3.400(o)(2)).

**Liberalizing legislation** — A law expanding benefits. Special effective date rules can apply.

**Retro / retroactive pay** — Lump sum back pay from effective date through the date your award goes into payment.

---

## Special compensation

**Service connection (SC)** — VA's recognition that a condition was caused or aggravated by service. Required for compensation.

**Direct service connection** — Condition caused by an in-service event/injury.

**Secondary service connection** — A new condition caused or aggravated by an existing service-connected condition (38 CFR § 3.310).

**Presumptive service connection** — VA presumes service connection for certain conditions in certain veterans (Agent Orange, Gulf War, PACT Act, etc.).

**Aggravation** — Pre-existing condition made worse by service.

---

## TDIU and SMC

**TDIU** — Total Disability Individual Unemployability. Pays at the 100% rate when service-connected disabilities prevent substantially gainful employment, even if your schedular rating is less than 100%. (38 CFR § 4.16)

**Schedular 100%** — Reaching 100% on the rating schedule itself, without TDIU.

**Marginal employment** — Income below the federal poverty threshold; doesn't count against TDIU.

**Protected work environment** — Self-employment or sheltered work doesn't count as substantially gainful employment for TDIU purposes (38 CFR § 4.16(a)).

**SMC** — Special Monthly Compensation. Higher rates for severe disabilities, loss of use of body parts, aid & attendance, housebound status, etc. Coded SMC-K, L, M, N, O, P, R, S, T.

**Aid & Attendance (A&A)** — Higher SMC rate for veterans needing help with daily activities.

**Housebound (HB)** — SMC rate for veterans substantially confined to home.

---

## Status indicators

**P&T** — Permanent and Total. 100% rating + permanent (no future re-exams). Unlocks dependent benefits, CHAMPVA, state property tax exemptions, etc.

**Total but not permanent** — 100% rating but VA expects improvement; future re-exams scheduled.

**Static disability** — Condition not expected to improve; usually no future re-exams.

**Protected rating** — Once a rating has been in place 5, 10, or 20 years, it's harder for VA to reduce.

---

## Procedural terms

**ITF** — Intent to File. Form 21-0966.

**C&P exam** — Compensation & Pension exam. VA-ordered medical exam to evaluate your claim.

**DBQ** — Disability Benefits Questionnaire. The clinical form examiners fill out; your private provider can also complete one.

**C-file** — Claims File. The complete VA file containing all your service records, medical records, and decision documents.

**STR** — Service Treatment Record. Medical records from your time in service.

**Duty to assist** — VA's obligation to help you develop your claim by ordering exams and pulling federal records (38 USC § 5103A).

**Benefit of the doubt** — When evidence is in equipoise (roughly equal), the law requires VA to decide in the veteran's favor (38 CFR § 4.3).

**Equipoise** — Evidence that's roughly equal for and against the veteran. Triggers benefit of the doubt.

## Appeal lanes (AMA)

The Appeals Modernization Act (AMA, 2019) created three "lanes":

1. **HLR (20-0996)** — Higher-Level Review. Same evidence, fresh look.
2. **Supplemental (20-0995)** — New and relevant evidence.
3. **Notice of Disagreement (10182)** — Direct appeal to the Board of Veterans' Appeals (BVA), with three sub-options:
   - Direct Review (no new evidence, no hearing)
   - Evidence Submission (new evidence, no hearing)
   - Hearing Request (with or without new evidence)

---

## Representation

**Accredited representative** — Authorized to represent you with VA. Three types:
- **VSO** — Veterans Service Organization (free)
- **Claims Agent** — Non-attorney (paid)
- **Attorney** — Licensed lawyer (paid; capped at 20% of retro by federal law)

**38 USC § 5901–5905** — Federal statute restricting paid representation to accredited reps.

**POA** — Power of Attorney. Form 21-22 (VSO) or 21-22a (attorney/agent).

---

## Money concepts

**Cost-of-living adjustment (COLA)** — Annual rate increase, effective Dec 1.

**Combined disability rating** — See "VA math" — uses 38 CFR § 4.25, not simple addition.

**Bilateral factor** — Bonus added when both sides of a paired extremity (left/right) are rated.

**Concurrent receipt** — Receiving both VA disability and military retirement (CRDP/CRSC).

**Backpay window** — From effective date to the date VA puts you in payment.
