# Higher-Level Review (HLR) Walkthrough — Form 20-0996

> **Educational use only. Not legal or claims advice.**
> *VVV Digitals · vvvdigitals.com*

---

## When to use HLR

You file an HLR when **you believe VA misapplied the law or misweighed the evidence already in your file**. No new evidence is allowed in the HLR lane. The senior reviewer looks at the same record and makes a fresh decision.

**Use HLR when:**
- You disagree with how a regulation was applied
- You think VA missed evidence already in your file
- You spot a math error in the rating combination
- You want to challenge an effective date based on existing evidence

**Do NOT use HLR when:**
- You have new medical records, lay statements, or buddy letters → file Supplemental Claim instead
- The decision is more than 1 year old without a continuing claim chain → CUE territory; consult an accredited rep

---

## Filing window

- Generally **1 year from the date of the decision** you're disputing
- Filing within 1 year **preserves your effective date**
- Filing after 1 year may forfeit the effective date

---

## What you'll need

- [ ] A copy of the rating decision you're disputing
- [ ] The decision date (usually on the cover letter)
- [ ] The specific issue(s) you want reviewed
- [ ] Your reasoning for each issue (legal or factual error)

## Filling out Form 20-0996

### Section I — Veteran Information
Standard demographic fields. Match exactly what's in VA's system. If you're a representative, complete Section I-B.

### Section II — Decision being reviewed
- Box A: Date of the decision letter (not the date you received it)
- Box B: List each contention you want reviewed. Be specific. Don't say "I disagree with everything."

### Section III — Type of review
Two options:
- **Direct review** — paper review only, no conference
- **Informal conference** — 30-minute call with the senior reviewer (RECOMMENDED)

> **TIP:** Always check informal conference. Even if your case is clean, a phone call with a senior reviewer beats paper-only review nine times out of ten. You can clarify points, hear their reasoning in real time, and respond.

### Section IV — Identify the error
This is the most important section. Be specific about the legal or factual error. Examples:

**Legal error example:**
> "VA failed to apply 38 CFR § 4.7 (rule for resolving doubt in favor of higher rating). My exam findings of [X] support a higher rating under DC [Y]."

**Factual error example:**
> "The April 2024 C&P examiner's report contains observations on pages 3–4 documenting [specific symptoms] that meet the criteria for [X]% under DC [Y]. The rating decision overlooked these findings."

> **TIP:** Cite specific page numbers, exam dates, and regulation sections. Vague disagreement loses; surgical disagreement wins.

### Section V — Signature
Date and sign. If you have a representative, they sign too.

## After filing

1. **Confirmation** — VA acknowledges receipt within ~30 days
2. **Conference scheduling** — if you opted in, you'll get a phone slot within 60–120 days
3. **Decision** — typical turnaround: 4–7 months (varies by RO)
4. **Outcome** — three possibilities:
   - Granted as requested (effective date preserved)
   - Granted in part
   - Denied — your next move is Board of Veterans' Appeals (BVA) or Supplemental Claim with new evidence

---

## What to bring to the informal conference

- The original decision letter
- Your Form 20-0996 (have your stated errors in front of you)
- Page numbers and exact quotes from records you're citing
- A short list of questions for the reviewer
- A pen and paper for notes

The reviewer is on the clock. **Lead with your strongest argument.**

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Submitting new evidence.** Anything new gets ignored on HLR. File a Supplemental Claim instead.
2. **Vague disagreement.** "I deserve more" is not a legal or factual error.
3. **Missing the 1-year window.** Set a calendar reminder the day you receive any rating decision.
4. **Not requesting the conference.** It's free and dramatically improves outcomes.

## Sample cover statement

You can attach a separate statement to your 20-0996. Here's a starter template:

> **Re: Higher-Level Review Request — [Your Name], File Number [X]**
>
> I am requesting a Higher-Level Review of the rating decision dated [DATE] regarding [ISSUE].
>
> **Issue 1: [Specific contention]**
>
> The decision states [QUOTE]. However, the evidence of record shows [SPECIFIC EVIDENCE WITH PAGE NUMBERS]. This appears to be a [legal/factual] error because [REASONING WITH REGULATION CITATION].
>
> **Requested action:** [SPECIFIC OUTCOME — e.g., "increase rating from 30% to 50% under DC 9411 with effective date of February 10, 2025"]
>
> [Repeat for each issue]
>
> Respectfully submitted,
> [Signature, date]
